## SAMPLE PROMPT 1 — Overdue Invoice Follow-Up (ONE-SHOT)
*One input. All three collection emails at once — separated and ready to send.*

```
You are a professional bookkeeper writing collection emails for an overdue invoice.

Client details:
- Client Name: [Client Name]
- Invoice Number: [Invoice #]
- Invoice Amount: $[Amount]
- Due Date: [Due Date]
- Days Overdue: [Number] days
- Service Provided: [Brief description, e.g., "monthly bookkeeping for March"]
- My Business Name: [Your Business Name]

Write all THREE follow-up emails at once, separated by dividers. Use the exact tone described for each stage.

---

EMAIL 1 — Gentle Reminder (Days 1–7 overdue)
Tone: Warm, assume it slipped through. Offer easy ways to pay. No guilt.

---

EMAIL 2 — Firm Follow-Up (Days 8–21 overdue)
Tone: Direct and professional. Reference that Email 1 was sent. State a clear deadline. Mention late fee policy if applicable: [Late fee policy, e.g., "1.5% per month" or "none"].

---

EMAIL 3 — Final Notice (Days 22+ overdue)
Tone: Serious and formal. State this is the final notice before collections or service suspension. Keep it factual, not emotional.

---

Format each email with: Subject line, greeting, body paragraphs, and sign-off.
My email signature: [Your Name, Title, Phone, Website]
```

## SAMPLE PROMPT 2 — Monthly P&L Summary Email (Plain English)
*Turn numbers into client-ready language — no jargon.*

```
You are a bookkeeper writing a monthly financial summary email for a business owner client.

Details:
- Client Name / Business Name: [Client Name]
- Reporting Period: [Month, Year]
- Total Revenue: $[Amount]
- Total Expenses: $[Amount]
- Net Profit (or Loss): $[Amount]
- Notable revenue items: [e.g., "Service revenue up 12% vs. last month" / "One-time project fee of $X included"]
- Notable expense items: [e.g., "Payroll increased due to new hire" / "Equipment purchase of $X" / "No unusual items"]
- Cash position: [e.g., "Bank balance as of month-end: $X" / "Receivables outstanding: $X"]
- Any concern or positive trend to highlight: [e.g., "Margins improved" / "AR aging needs attention" / "None"]
- My Business Name: [Your Business Name]
- My Name & Title: [Your Name, e.g., Jane Smith, Certified Bookkeeper]

Write a monthly P&L summary email that:
1. Opens with a brief, friendly summary of the month overall (1–2 sentences)
2. Presents the key numbers clearly (revenue / expenses / net profit)
3. Explains the notable items in plain language a non-accountant can understand
4. Highlights the cash position
5. Flags any concern or positive trend with a clear next step or observation
6. Closes with an offer to discuss further

Subject line + full email. Plain English only — no accounting jargon unless absolutely necessary, and define it if used.
```
