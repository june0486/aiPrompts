## SAMPLE PROMPT 1 — Job Description Generator
*Any role, any industry. Structured to attract strong candidates — not just list requirements.*

```
You are an experienced HR professional. Write a compelling, inclusive job description for an open position.

Details:
- Job Title: [Job Title]
- Company Name: [Company Name]
- Department: [Department]
- Location: [City, State / Remote / Hybrid]
- Employment Type: [Full-time / Part-time / Contract]
- Salary Range: $[Min]–$[Max] per year (or "competitive, based on experience" if not disclosing)
- Reports To: [Title of Manager]

Responsibilities (list 5–8 key duties):
[List them here]

Requirements (must-have):
[List 3–5 required qualifications]

Preferred qualifications (nice-to-have):
[List 1–3 preferred items, or "none"]

Company culture in 2–3 words: [e.g., "collaborative, fast-paced, mission-driven"]
Any DEI or EEO statement to include: [Yes — use standard EEO boilerplate / No]

Write a complete job description with:
1. A compelling opening paragraph about the role and company
2. Responsibilities section (bulleted)
3. Requirements section (bulleted — separate must-have from preferred)
4. What you offer (benefits, culture, growth opportunity)
5. Standard EEO statement if requested

Tone: Professional and engaging — this should attract strong candidates, not just list requirements.
```

## SAMPLE PROMPT 2 — Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
*The document HR professionals dread writing most — structured, complete, legally careful.*

```
You are an HR professional drafting a formal Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).

Details:
- Employee Name: [Employee Name]
- Job Title: [Job Title]
- Department: [Department]
- Manager Name & Title: [Manager Name, Title]
- HR Representative (if applicable): [Name / "N/A"]
- PIP Start Date: [Date]
- PIP Duration: [e.g., 30 / 60 / 90 days]
- Review Date(s): [Intermediate check-in date(s) and final review date]

Performance concerns (be specific — list 2–4 measurable issues):
[e.g., "Sales quota attainment at 52% vs. required 80% for the past 3 months" / "Three documented instances of missing project deadlines in Q3" / "Multiple customer complaints regarding responsiveness"]

Specific, measurable improvement goals:
[e.g., "Achieve 80% of monthly sales quota for each month of the PIP period" / "Submit all project deliverables on or before deadline with no exceptions" / "Zero customer escalations related to responsiveness"]

Support and resources the company will provide:
[e.g., "Weekly 1:1 with manager every Monday" / "Access to sales coaching program" / "Reduced account load for first 30 days" / "None beyond standard management support"]

Consequence of non-improvement:
[e.g., "Failure to meet the goals outlined above may result in further disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment"]

State where the employee works: [State]

Draft a complete, formal PIP document that includes:
1. Introduction and purpose of the PIP
2. Summary of performance concerns (specific, documented, measurable)
3. Required improvement goals with clear, measurable standards
4. Timeline and review checkpoints
5. Support and resources provided by the company
6. Consequence statement
7. Signature lines for employee, manager, and HR

⚠️ Include a reminder at the top: "Review with employment counsel before issuing — PIP requirements vary by state."
```
