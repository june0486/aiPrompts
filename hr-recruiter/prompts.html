ChatGPT Prompts for HR Managers & Recruiters

25 Copy-Paste Templates | Hiring · Interviews · Performance Management · HR Admin

⚠️ STATE EMPLOYMENT LAW NOTICE (applies to termination letters, PIPs, and FMLA documents): Employment laws vary significantly by state. Documents in this pack — particularly termination letters, Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs), and leave notices — must comply with the specific laws of the state where the employee works.

States with stricter employment regulations require extra attention:

  • California: Final pay due same day as termination; WARN Act thresholds lower than federal; at-will exceptions broader; specific PIP and documentation standards apply.
  • New York: Wage Theft Prevention Act notice requirements; NYC additional protections; specific timing for final pay by termination type.
  • New Jersey, Massachusetts, Washington, Illinois: Additional notice, pay timing, and anti-discrimination protections that may exceed federal defaults.

Always have termination letters and PIPs reviewed by an HR attorney or employment counsel familiar with the applicable state's laws before issuing to employees. This pack provides drafting assistance only — not legal advice.

Prompt 1 — Job Description Generator

You are an experienced HR professional. Write a compelling, inclusive job description for an open position.

Details:
- Job Title: [Job Title]
- Company Name: [Company Name]
- Department: [Department]
- Location: [City, State / Remote / Hybrid]
- Employment Type: [Full-time / Part-time / Contract]
- Salary Range: $[Min]–$[Max] per year (or "competitive, based on experience" if not disclosing)
- Reports To: [Title of Manager]

Responsibilities (list 5–8 key duties):
[List them here, e.g., "Manage daily accounts payable / Lead weekly team standups / Own the quarterly reporting process"]

Requirements (must-have):
[List 3–5 required qualifications, e.g., "3+ years of experience in X / Bachelor's degree in Y / Proficiency in Z software"]

Preferred qualifications (nice-to-have):
[List 1–3 preferred items, or "none"]

Company culture in 2–3 words: [e.g., "collaborative, fast-paced, mission-driven"]
Any DEI or EEO statement to include: [Yes — use standard EEO boilerplate / No]

Write a complete job description with:
1. A compelling opening paragraph about the role and company
2. Responsibilities section (bulleted)
3. Requirements section (bulleted — separate must-have from preferred)
4. What you offer (benefits, culture, growth opportunity)
5. Standard EEO statement if requested

Tone: Professional and engaging — this should attract strong candidates, not just list requirements.

CATEGORY 2 — Interviewing & Evaluation (6 Prompts)

Prompt 8 — Behavioral Interview Questions Generator (STAR Method)

You are an experienced HR professional. Generate a set of behavioral interview questions for a specific role using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

Details:
- Role: [Job Title]
- Key competencies to assess (choose 4–6): [e.g., "communication, problem-solving, leadership, adaptability, teamwork, conflict resolution, attention to detail, customer focus"]
- Seniority level: [Entry / Mid / Senior / Manager / Director]
- Industry context (if relevant): [e.g., "healthcare / tech startup / retail / finance" or "general"]
- Any specific past issues with hires in this role (if any): [e.g., "previous hires struggled with handling ambiguity" or "none"]

For each competency, write:
1. The competency being assessed (label it clearly)
2. One primary behavioral question using STAR framing ("Tell me about a time when...")
3. Two follow-up probing questions to dig deeper if the candidate gives a vague answer
4. What a strong answer looks like (1–2 sentences — the "green flags")
5. What a weak answer looks like (1–2 sentences — the "red flags")

Total: 4–6 competencies × 3 questions each. Format cleanly so the interviewer can print and use it in the room.

Prompt 9 — Reference Check Question Script

You are an HR professional. Write a reference check question script for a specific role.

Details:
- Role being hired for: [Job Title]
- Reference's likely relationship to candidate: [e.g., "former direct manager / former peer / former direct report"]
- Key areas to probe: [e.g., "work quality, reliability, how they handle pressure, collaboration, reason for leaving"]
- Any specific concern to probe carefully (optional): [e.g., "candidate mentioned a conflict with leadership" / "we noticed a short tenure at their last company" / "none"]

Write a reference check script that includes:
1. An opening script (who you are, why you're calling, permission to take notes)
2. 8–10 structured questions covering:
   - Role, responsibilities, and context (verify the resume)
   - Performance and work quality
   - How they work with others
   - How they handle stress, feedback, and adversity
   - The specific concern area (frame diplomatically)
   - Would they rehire? (the most important question — always ask this last)
3. A graceful close and thank-you

Include the exact phrasing for the "would you rehire?" question and one follow-up if the answer is hesitant. Format as a printable script.

Prompt 14 — Annual Performance Review (Strengths Focus)

You are an HR professional or manager writing an annual performance review for an employee.

Details:
- Employee Name: [Employee Name]
- Job Title: [Job Title]
- Review Period: [e.g., January 2024 – December 2024]
- Manager Name & Title: [Your Name, Your Title]
- Overall Performance Rating: [e.g., Exceeds Expectations / Meets Expectations / Below Expectations]
- Top 3–4 accomplishments this year: [List with specifics, e.g., "led migration to new CRM — completed on time and under budget / reduced customer response time by 32%"]
- Core strengths demonstrated: [e.g., "exceptional communication, takes initiative, mentors junior team members"]
- One growth area (stated positively): [e.g., "continues to develop delegation skills as team grows"]
- Goal or development focus for next year: [e.g., "lead a cross-functional project / achieve PMP certification / take on people management responsibilities"]

Write a performance review narrative that:
1. Opens with an overall assessment statement (positive, specific)
2. Highlights 3–4 accomplishments with specific impact where possible
3. Describes core strengths with behavioral examples
4. Addresses the growth area constructively — not as a weakness, but as a next-level opportunity
5. Closes with forward-looking goals and confidence in the employee

Length: 300–400 words. Tone: Affirming, specific, professional. Avoid generic filler phrases like "great team player" without examples.

Length: 300–400 words. Tone: Professional, direct, fair — not punitive. This document may be used in legal proceedings — write factually, not emotionally.

Prompt 16 — 90-Day Check-In Template

Format as a fillable document with space for notes. Manager completes Section 1 before the meeting; Section 2 is discussed together; Section 3 is filled in together.
